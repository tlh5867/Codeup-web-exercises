Arches National Park

  • The total size of Arches National Park is more than 76,000 acres. It was first made available to visitors in 1923. Now, more than 1 million arrive each year.
  • Arches National Park trails are virtually overrun with stone arches; in total, there are over 2,000 recorded.
  • Arches National Park history can be traced back over 10,000 years. This is when the first signs of humans appeared as signs carved into indigenous rock formations.
  • In 1940, the Skyline Arch was formed through a sudden shift. While the huge, 60-foot Delicate Arch took much longer for nature to create.

Big Bend National Park

  • About 75 million years ago, a 40-mile-wide trough sank along fault lines which resulted in the Santa Elena Canyon cliffs and the Sierra del Carmen.
  • Langford Hot Springs near the park’s Rio Grande was formed by volcanic activity is about 105 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • The Chisos Mountains were formed nearly 35 millions years ago after volcanic activity spewed layers of ash and molten rock squeezed up through the ground.
  • The Milky Way rises in Big Bend during sunrise.

Carlsbad Caverns National Park

  • The land that forms Carlsbad Caverns was once part of an ancient underwater reef called Capitan Reef.
  • The caverns were forged by sulfuric acid – not water erosion, as is the case with most limestone caves.
  • Before 1925, guests who visited the park were lowered into the caves in a big bucket.
